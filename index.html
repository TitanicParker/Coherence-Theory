Coherence Theory

Early work by Liam Moloney (TitanicParker)

A geometric account of what it means for something to make sense.

What Coherence Theory asks

Coherence Theory starts from a simple question:

When we say we “get” something, what has to be happening, structurally?

The working assumption is that coherence is not a vague feeling, but the result of a small number of repeatable moves that can be described geometrically.

The core picture (very high level)

In this project, coherence is modelled as a finite set of operators and invariants.

Operators

Operators are basic structural moves that understanding can make. In its current form, Coherence Theory assumes six of them, playing roles of roughly this kind:

  • opening a distinction,
  • expanding or elaborating that structure,
  • relating parts,
  • correcting or realigning,
  • integrating into a whole,
  • settling or closing.

Invariants and the 6×3×3 structure

When these operators act, they do not produce an unlimited variety of outcomes. They stabilise in a finite set of invariants—repeatable landing patterns of coherence that feel “in place”.

The current working architecture is:

  • 6 operators,
  • each with 3 submodes,
  • each submode with 3 invariants.

This gives 6 × 3 × 3 = 54 invariants.

6 operators 3 submodes each 3 invariants per submode 54 invariant forms of coherence

The detailed list of invariants, their names, and worked examples is being developed separately and will be published from this repository as it stabilises.

Why sentences?

Sentences are treated here as the simplest observable traces of coherence. Whenever a sentence “makes sense”, something structural has already taken place:

  • a distinction has been opened,
  • possibilities have been expanded and constrained,
  • relations have been set up,
  • misfits have been corrected,
  • and the whole has come to rest in a stable pattern.

Coherence Theory reads each complete sentence as a finite walk through its operator–invariant structure. The long-term aim is to make this walk explicit enough that it can be studied, compared, and tested across many kinds of language.

What this repository is (now)

At this stage, the repository is deliberately minimal. It provides:

  • a custom license for this early material (LICENSE),
  • a short project overview (README.md),
  • this conceptual landing page (index.html),
  • and, in time, draft documents on operators, invariants, and sentence studies as they become stable enough to publish.

The emphasis is on clarity and correctness rather than completeness. Everything here should be read as a public draft, not a finished theory.

Direction of work

Ongoing work under the name Coherence Theory includes:

  • sharpening the geometric description of the six operators,
  • articulating and naming the 54 invariants,
  • developing a method for walking individual sentences through the structure,
  • and collecting example sentence studies that can be checked and repeated by others.

As these pieces are clarified, they will be introduced gradually into this repository.
